Honours Thesis

University of Technology Sydney

As an honours student in 2022, I had the privilege of conducting research under the supervision of Professor Paul Kennedy and Associate Professor Daniel Catchpoole, focusing on the development of a neural network for the classification of Rhabdomyosarcoma, a rare childhood tumor, based on histological features. Titled "Explaining How Deep Learning Models Classify Childhood Tumours Based on Histological Features," my thesis aimed to bridge the gap between medical diagnosis and artificial intelligence, with the ultimate goal of influencing treatment plans and identifying subgroups within this complex disease.

Throughout the project, I meticulously worked towards several key objectives. This included collecting and pre-processing rhabdomyosarcoma datasets to ensure data quality and consistency, building and validating a neural network capable of classifying the tumor into major histological groups with accuracy comparable to pathologists, and extending the model's capabilities to detect anomalies and locate new patterns.

Collaborating closely with my supervisors, histopathologists, and fellow students, I engaged in weekly meetings to discuss progress, share insights, and refine methodologies. Leveraging cutting-edge techniques in deep learning, I developed a convolutional neural network (CNN) to classify histological samples into subtypes of rhabdomyosarcoma, while also exploring Explainable AI methods to interpret the model's decisions.

The culmination of my research efforts was reflected in the successful completion of my thesis, which achieved a grade of 92%. Additionally, I had the opportunity to present my findings at a Children's Cancer Research Unit seminar, where I effectively communicated complex computer science concepts to a multidisciplinary audience of biologists, showcasing my ability to bridge the gap between diverse fields of study.
